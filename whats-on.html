grace

August 28-31 at 8.00pm


Brookyn jazz star Grace Penrose is bringing her musical talents back to Ireland this month. Dubbed a saxophonist prodigy at age 12, Grace has traveled the world, playing more than 600 concerts at venues ranging from The Jazz Café Dublin to the San Quentin Prison in California. She’s studied under saxophone legends Lee Konitz and Phil Woods, played for the Boston Pops’ Keith Lockhart, and released her 10th album, Trying to Figure It Out, in February.

beata
hlavenkova

September 1-3 at 7.30pm


This phenomenal Czech pianist, composer, arranger and producer is widely recognised as one of the most sought-after and genre-free jazz musicians in Europe. Nominated for a Czech “Grammy Award” for her first album Joy for Joel, she won the award twice for her collaborative projects with Lenka Dusilova. Beata won the Best Czech Jazz and Blues Album award of 2013 for her second independent project, Theodoros, which also received top honours in the Concept Album category at the 14th annual Independent Music Awards.

September 15-17 at 8.00pm


Since its beginnings in the late 1990s, Hot Club of Cowtown’s star has continues to rise as its reputation for jaw-dropping virtuosity and unforgettable live shows has become the band’s brand. The band is made up of fiddle, guitar, and bass and plays a combination of hot jazz and Western swing. This energetic swing trio are truly exciting to watch live, injecting each song with a genuine, upbeat charm and spice that makes you melt and fall in love with the music.

jason marsalis
vibes

22-24

jason marsalis
vibes

September 22-24 at 7.30pm


Not just vibraphone, but glockenspiel, xylophone, marimba and even tubular bells. The youngest of the remarkable Marsalis brothers plays them all, sometimes multi-tracking several at once. This is an engaging set, tuneful and good-humoured, with titles like Blessed Unrest and The Man With Two Left Feet. Marsalis has a wonderfully crisp technique and a flair for inventing catchy melodies. As for the title number, you’ve never heard When the Saints Go Marching In sound anything like this. The rest of the quartet – pianist Austin Johnson, bassist Will Goble and drummer David Potter – really shine here.

September 29-30 at 7.30pm


As one of the most influential jazz guitarists in the world, Kurt Rosenwinkel’s playing has been dissected, transcribed, and imitated by hordes of aspiring young guitar players at music schools everywhere. As one of jazz's foremost artists, Kurt Rosenwinkel has established a reputation as an innovator and constant seeker on the guitar. He has carved out a unique sound over many years of experiment.

4-7

sheryl
bailey

September 4-7 at 7.30pm


There’s no description more apt for New York star Sheryl Bailey than “A sizzling guitar goddess”, coined by Elliot Simon of All About Jazz. Sheryl’s playing is unquestionably “sizzling”. She has an incredible guitar technique and impeccable musical taste. It’s said that she can “go from zero to blazing in two beats”, but she is continually praised for never sacrificing melody and lyricism. Sheryl has performed and recorded with top names all over the globe including Richard Bona, George Garzone and Ken Peplowski. To date she has recorded eight albums and has been an Artist in Residence and a Clinician at countless other programs and institutions in the USA.

6-8

sup trio &
strings

October 6-8 at 7.30pm


This is the latest innovative contemporary jazz group led by Cormac McCarthy, the renowned composer, arranger and pianist from Cork. The Trio successfully represented Ireland at the recent Euroradio Jazz event in Amsterdam. His compositions and arrangements have been recorded by such jazz icons as Phil Woods and Jeff Hamilton. As a pianist, he has performed extensively at concert venues, clubs and festivals throughout Europe and the US. With his new Sup Trio project, Cormac continues to explore the many facets of jazz and other disciplines working with an ensemble comprised of top rank musicians based in Cork City.
